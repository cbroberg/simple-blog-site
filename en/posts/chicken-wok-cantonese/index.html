Chicken Wok — The Classic Cantonese Stir-Fry

January 31, 2025 · Editorial

What is chicken wok?

Chicken wok is one of the most beloved dishes in Chinese cuisine — and for good reason. The dish combines tender strips of chicken with crisp vegetables in an aromatic sauce, all cooked over high heat in just a few minutes. The result is a dish full of flavour, colour and texture.

The dish you see in the photo is a classic Cantonese stir-fry with chicken, spinach, carrots, pointed cabbage and spring onions in an orange-red sauce — served with noodles.

The history of wok cooking

Wok cooking originates from China and has roots going back more than 2,000 years. The round, deep pan — the wok — emerged in southern China during the Han Dynasty (206 BC – 220 AD), when fuel was expensive and scarce. The high heat and rapid cooking was not just practical — it was a necessity.

In the Canton region (today Guangdong Province in southern China), a special food culture developed based on fresh ingredients, light saucing and minimal cooking time. Cantonese cuisine is today considered one of the four great culinary traditions in China.

"China is the homeland of cooking — and the wok is its most important tool."

Facts about Guangdong (Canton)

  • 🌍 Location: Southern China, bordering Hong Kong and Macau
  • 👥 Population: Approx. 130 million people
  • 🍜 Food tradition: Cantonese cuisine is the most widespread Chinese cuisine outside China
  • 🥢 Characteristics: Fresh ingredients, light flavour, minimal seasoning — the natural taste of the ingredients is emphasised
  • 🏙️ Largest city: Guangzhou (formerly Canton)
  • 🌶️ Sauce use: Oyster sauce, hoisin sauce and light sweet-sour sauce are typical of the region

Recipe: Chicken Wok with Vegetables and Noodles

Serves 2–3 | Preparation time: 25 minutes

Ingredients

For the wok:

  • 400 g chicken breast — sliced into thin strips
  • 2 carrots — peeled and sliced
  • 150 g fresh spinach
  • ¼ pointed cabbage — roughly shredded
  • 2 spring onions — sliced diagonally
  • 3 cloves garlic — minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger — grated
  • 3 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp hoisin sauce
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp neutral oil for frying
  • Salt and pepper

To serve:

  • 200 g egg noodles or rice noodles
  • Sesame seeds and spring onions to garnish

Method

  1. Prepare the ingredients: Cut the chicken into thin strips and season with a little salt and pepper. Prep all the vegetables.
  2. Cook the noodles according to the packet instructions. Set aside.
  3. Heat the wok over high heat until it starts to smoke lightly. Add the oil.
  4. Fry the chicken for 3–4 minutes until golden and cooked through. Remove and set aside.
  5. Stir-fry the vegetables: Add garlic and ginger for 30 seconds, then carrots and cabbage for 2 minutes. Add spinach and spring onions last.
  6. Add the sauce: Pour over the oyster sauce, hoisin sauce, soy sauce and sesame oil. Stir well.
  7. Return the chicken to the wok and combine everything for 1 minute.
  8. Serve immediately over the noodles with sesame seeds and extra spring onions.

Tips for the perfect wok

  • 🔥 High heat is key — the wok should be hot enough to smoke lightly
  • ⏱️ Cook quickly — too long in the pan gives soft, soggy vegetables
  • 🥄 Mise en place — have everything ready before you start, it moves fast!
  • 🍳 Cook in batches — too much in the wok lowers the temperature
